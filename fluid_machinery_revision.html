UNIT 1: Impact of Jet & Hydraulic Turbines

Theory Derivations Numericals

1. Impact of Jet

Force exerted by jet = Rate of change of momentum = ṁ × ΔV
Force on Fixed Vertical Plate: F = ρAV²
Force on Fixed Inclined Plate (angle θ): F_n = ρAV² sinθ (normal to plate)
Force on Moving Flat Plate: F = ρA(V-u)²
Work Done on Series of Curved Vanes: W = ρAV_r(V_w1 + V_w2)u
V_r = V - u (relative velocity)
V_w1 = V, V_w2 = V_r cosβ - u
Why curved vanes preferred over flat plates?
  • Flat plate: η_max = 50%
  • Curved vane: η_max = 98%
  • Smooth change of momentum, less energy loss

2. Pelton Wheel (Impulse Turbine)

Maximum efficiency condition: u/V = 1/2
η_max = (1 + cosφ)/2 where φ = 180° - β (blade angle)
Pelton Wheel Formulas: Jet Velocity: V = C_v√(2gH)
Bucket Speed: u = πDN/60
Hydraulic Efficiency: η_h = 2(V-u)(1+kcosβ)u / V²
Power: P = ρQ(V-u)(1+kcosβ)u
Specific Speed: N_s = N√P / H^(5/4)
Number of Buckets: Z = 15 + D/2d (Tyson's formula)

Velocity Triangle for Pelton Wheel

Governing of Pelton Wheel:
  • Spear valve: Controls nozzle area → regulates discharge
  • Deflector: Diverts jet temporarily during load rejection
  • Servo motor: Operates spear and deflector

UNIT 2: Centrifugal Pumps

Theory Formulas Numericals

1. Classification of Centrifugal Pumps

Basis Types Application

Low: 10-50 (High head, Low Q)
Medium: 50-80
High: 80-160 (Low head, High Q)
Very High: 160-300 (Propeller type)

2. Work Done by Impeller (Euler's Equation)

Euler's Turbine Equation: H = (V_w2*u_2 - V_w1*u_1)/g
For radial entry (V_w1 = 0): H = V_w2*u_2/g
Work Done Formulas: Work per second: W = ρQ(V_w2*u_2 - V_w1*u_1)
For radial entry: W = ρQ*V_w2*u_2
Power: P = ρgQH/η_overall

Velocity Triangle for Centrifugal Pump

3. Efficiencies of Centrifugal Pump

Manometric Efficiency

η_man = gH_m / V_w2*u_2

Typical: 80-90%

Mechanical Efficiency

η_mech = ρgQH_m / P_shaft

Typical: 90-95%

Volumetric Efficiency

η_vol = Q / (Q + q)

Typical: 95-98%

Overall Efficiency

η_overall = η_man × η_mech × η_vol

Typical: 70-85%

4. Net Positive Suction Head (NPSH)

Condition for no cavitation: NPSHa ≥ NPSHr
NPSH Formula: NPSH = (P_atm/ρg) - (P_v/ρg) - h_s - h_fs

P_atm = Atmospheric pressure
P_v = Vapor pressure
h_s = Static suction lift
h_fs = Friction loss in suction pipe

5. Pumps in Series and Parallel

Series Connection

  • H_total = H₁ + H₂
  • Q_total = Q₁ = Q₂
  • Used for high head

Parallel Connection

  • H_total = H₁ = H₂
  • Q_total = Q₁ + Q₂
  • Used for high discharge

6. Cavitation

Cavitation: Formation and collapse of vapor bubbles when pressure falls below vapor pressure.

Effects: Pitting, erosion, noise, vibration, reduced efficiency

Prevention: Maintain NPSHa > NPSHr, reduce suction lift, use larger suction pipe

UNIT 3: Reciprocating Pumps & Special Pumps

Theory Derivations Numericals

1. Reciprocating Pump Theory

Positive displacement pump - delivers fixed volume per stroke
Discharge Formulas: Single Acting: Q_th = ALN/60
Double Acting: Q_th = 2ALN/60
Actual: Q_act = C_d × Q_th
Parameter Single Acting Double Acting
Discharge ALN/60 2ALN/60
Work per stroke ρgAL(H_s + H_d) 2ρgAL(H_s + H_d)

2. Slip and Coefficient of Discharge

Slip Formulas: Slip = Q_th - Q_act
% Slip = ((Q_th - Q_act)/Q_th) × 100
C_d = Q_act/Q_th (0.90 - 0.95)
Negative Slip: Q_act > Q_th
Occurs when delivery pipe is short and inertia head is high

3. Indicator Diagram

Ideal Indicator Diagram

4. Work Done by Reciprocating Pump

Work Done (without air vessels): W = ρgAL(H_s + H_d) + Acceleration work
W = ρgQH_m + ρA²L³N²/(3600a)
Work Done (with air vessels): W = ρgQH_m
Work Saved by Air Vessels: W_saved = ρA²L³N²/(3600a)
% Saved = (AL²N²)/(3600 × g × a × H_m) × 100

5. Comparison: Reciprocating vs Centrifugal Pump

Parameter Reciprocating Centrifugal

Priming Not required Required

6. Special Pumps

Gear Pump

  • Trapped between teeth
  • Fixed displacement
  • Self-priming
  • Good for viscous liquids

Vane Pump

  • Sliding vanes in rotor
  • Self-priming
  • Quiet operation
  • Good efficiency (85-95%)

Diaphragm Pump

  • Flexible diaphragm
  • No contact with liquid
  • Handles corrosive liquids
  • Gentle pumping

Jet Pump

  • Venturi effect
  • No moving parts
  • Low efficiency (30-40%)
  • Simple construction
